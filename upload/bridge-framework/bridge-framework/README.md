# 🪜 Bridge Framework Skill

## What This Skill Does

The Bridge Framework skill guides you through a structured methodology for crafting clear, compelling problem, opportunity, or situation statements that anchor strategic projects. It helps you move beyond surface-level symptoms to identify root causes, articulate consequences, and connect local issues to broader strategic priorities.

## Why Use This Skill

**Traditional problem statements often fail because they:**
- Jump to solutions too quickly
- Stay at symptom level without exploring root causes
- Lack urgency or emotional resonance
- Don't connect to broader strategic context

**This skill helps you create statements that:**
- Crystallize the real tension or opportunity
- Dig to structural root causes using the 5 Whys
- Make consequences of inaction tangible
- Link local issues to organizational mission
- Energize stakeholders with vivid, motivating language

## When to Use

Activate this skill when you need to:
- Kickoff a new strategic project
- Define the foundation for a business case
- Articulate a problem that needs executive attention
- Frame an emerging opportunity for capture
- Align teams around what really needs addressing
- Document the "why" behind an initiative

## What Makes This Different

### Structured Process
The skill follows a proven 5-phase methodology:
1. Choose entry point (Problem/Opportunity/Situation)
2. Surface observable tension
3. Apply 5 Whys for root cause
4. Articulate consequences of inaction
5. Connect to mission/vision

### Solution-Prevention
The skill actively prevents premature solution-jumping, keeping focus on deeply understanding the problem space first.

### Quality Enforcement
Every statement is validated against a comprehensive checklist ensuring it's specific, rooted in structural causes, consequence-aware, strategically connected, and motivating.

### Adaptive Questioning
The skill adjusts its questioning based on your responses, pushing deeper when answers are vague and moving efficiently when you're providing good detail.

## Framework Background

This skill is based on the Bridge Framework methodology used for strategic project planning. The framework recognizes that the quality of a solution is directly proportional to the quality of the problem definition - and that most failed initiatives suffer from unclear, unmotivating problem statements.

The "bridge" metaphor represents the journey from current state to desired state. The problem/opportunity/situation statement is the anchor point that makes the entire bridge stable.

## Tips for Best Results

### Be Specific
Replace "things aren't working well" with concrete examples like "three clients in the past month asked for features we don't offer."

### Embrace the 5 Whys
Don't resist when Claude keeps asking "why?" - this is where breakthrough insights happen. The fifth "why" usually reveals something structural.

### Name the Cost
Be honest about what's at stake. Generic costs ("it would be bad") don't motivate. Specific costs ("we lose $50K/month and the best engineer just gave notice") do.

### Connect Up
Always answer "why does this matter beyond this team/project?" The connection to mission reveals whether this is truly strategic.

### Don't Solve Yet
Resist the urge to propose solutions during this phase. You'll have better solutions once the problem is crystal clear.
