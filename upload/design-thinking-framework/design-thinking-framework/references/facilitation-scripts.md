# Facilitation Scripts

Word-for-word scripts for running design thinking workshops.

## Opening a Design Thinking Session

```
"Welcome everyone. Today we're going to use design thinking to tackle [problem].

Design thinking is about being human-centered—we start with people, not solutions.
We'll move through five phases: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test.

A few ground rules:
1. Defer judgment—all ideas welcome during ideation
2. Encourage wild ideas—they often lead to breakthroughs
3. Build on others' ideas—say 'yes, and...'
4. Stay focused on topic
5. One conversation at a time
6. Be visual—sketch, don't just talk
7. Go for quantity in ideation

Let's begin."
```

## Empathy Interview Facilitation

### Before the Interview
```
"You're about to interview a real user. Your job is to understand their world, not pitch solutions.

Key techniques:
- Ask 'why' five times
- Ask 'tell me about the last time...'
- Listen more than you talk
- Notice body language and emotions
- Follow the energy—if they light up, dig deeper
- Embrace silence—let them think

Avoid:
- Yes/no questions
- Leading questions
- Talking about your solution
- Interrupting

Ready? Let's practice one question first."
```

### Interview Opening Script
```
"Thank you so much for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm [name] and I'm working on understanding [topic] better.

This isn't a test—there are no right or wrong answers. I'm genuinely curious about your experience. Please be completely honest, even if you think it might be negative.

I'll be taking notes, and with your permission, I might record our conversation. Everything you share will be kept confidential.

Do you have any questions before we start?

Great. Let's begin with something easy—tell me about yourself and what you do."
```

## Empathy Map Facilitation

```
"Now we're going to synthesize what we learned into an Empathy Map.

[Draw the four-quadrant template on whiteboard]

Let's focus on [persona name] based on our research.

THINK & FEEL: What occupies their thinking? What really matters to them?
[Give 3 minutes for sticky notes]

SAY & DO: What did we hear them say? What behaviors did we observe?
[Give 3 minutes for sticky notes]

SEE: What do they see in their environment? Who influences them?
[Give 3 minutes for sticky notes]

HEAR: What do they hear from friends, colleagues, media?
[Give 3 minutes for sticky notes]

Now let's identify:
PAINS: What are their frustrations, fears, obstacles?
GAINS: What do they want, need, hope for?

[Give 5 minutes for both]

Let's cluster and discuss patterns."
```

## POV Statement Facilitation

```
"We're going to create a Point of View statement that frames our design challenge.

The format is: [USER] needs [NEED] because [INSIGHT].

Let's break this down:

USER—be specific, not 'a user' but 'a busy parent with two kids under 5'
NEED—use a verb, not a solution. Not 'needs an app' but 'needs to coordinate schedules'
INSIGHT—the surprising 'because' from our research

Take 5 minutes individually to write your POV statement.

[After 5 minutes]

Let's share. Read your statement, then we'll discuss which elements are strongest.

[After sharing]

Now let's craft our team POV. Which user framing resonates most? Which need? Which insight?"
```

## How Might We Facilitation

```
"Now we'll generate How Might We questions from our POV.

HMW questions reframe problems as opportunities. They should be:
- Not too broad (can't ideate)
- Not too narrow (solution embedded)
- Inspiring and actionable

Starting with our POV: [read POV]

I'll give you techniques. For each, write one HMW on a sticky note.

1. AMP UP THE GOOD: What's working that we could amplify?
   'How might we...' [30 seconds]

2. REMOVE THE BAD: What pain point could we eliminate?
   'How might we...' [30 seconds]

3. EXPLORE THE OPPOSITE: What if we made the annoying thing delightful?
   'How might we...' [30 seconds]

4. QUESTION ASSUMPTIONS: What if we removed a constraint?
   'How might we...' [30 seconds]

5. CHANGE TIME: What if it happened earlier or later?
   'How might we...' [30 seconds]

6. BREAK INTO PIECES: What's one part we could address?
   'How might we...' [30 seconds]

[After generation]

Post your HMWs on the wall. Take 2 minutes to read everyone's silently.

Now everyone gets 3 dots. Vote for the HMWs that excite you most.

[After voting]

Our top HMW for ideation is: [read winner]"
```

## Crazy 8s Facilitation

```
"Now we're going to generate lots of ideas quickly using Crazy 8s.

[Distribute paper, have participants fold into 8 sections]

Here are the rules:
- 8 ideas in 8 minutes
- 1 minute per box
- No talking
- No erasing
- Ugly sketches are fine
- Quantity over quality

Your HMW: [read the selected HMW]

When I say go, you have 1 minute for your first idea. I'll call time for each box.

Sketch AND write a title for each idea.

Ready? Go!

[Call out each minute, count down last 10 seconds]

Time! Put your pens down.

Now circle your favorite 2 ideas. Star any wild card that's weird but interesting.

Let's go around and share your top idea. You have 1 minute each. No feedback yet—just share."
```

## Idea Voting Facilitation

```
"Time to select ideas for prototyping.

Everyone has 3 large dot stickers and 1 small star sticker.

Large dots = ideas you think we should pursue
Star = 'supervote' for one idea you feel strongest about

Vote silently. You can put multiple dots on one idea if you feel strongly.

You cannot vote for your own ideas.

[After voting]

Let's count. The ideas with most votes are:
1. [Idea] - [X votes]
2. [Idea] - [X votes]
3. [Idea] - [X votes]

Does anyone want to make a case for an idea that didn't get votes?

[Allow brief discussion]

We'll prototype: [selected idea]. Let's storyboard how it works."
```

## Prototype Introduction

```
"Remember: we're building to think, not building to ship.

Prototypes help us:
- Make ideas tangible
- Test assumptions
- Fail fast and cheap
- Learn from users

Fidelity should match our learning goal:
- Paper/sketches for flow and concept
- Clickable wireframes for interaction
- Role play for services
- Fake landing pages for value proposition

Our question to answer: [specific question]

Therefore, our prototype should be: [type and fidelity]

You have [X hours]. Focus on the critical path that answers our question.
Don't build everything—build just enough to test."
```

## User Testing Introduction

```
"We're about to test our prototype with real users.

Remember: we're testing the prototype, not the person.

Key principles:
- Give tasks, not tours ('Please try to...' not 'Here you can...')
- Stay quiet and observe
- Don't help—let them struggle
- Ask 'what did you expect?' when they're confused
- No defending the design

Capture:
- What worked
- What confused them
- What surprised you
- Direct quotes

After each test, we'll do a quick debrief:
1. What did we learn?
2. What should we change for the next test?

Ready? First user arriving in 5 minutes."
```

## Session Closing

```
"Let's reflect on what we accomplished today.

In our EMPATHY phase, we learned: [key insight]

We DEFINED our challenge as: [POV statement]

Through IDEATION, we generated [X] ideas and selected: [winning concept]

We PROTOTYPED: [description]

Through TESTING, we validated: [validated learning]

And we learned: [key learning/surprise]

Our next steps:
1. [Immediate action]
2. [Follow-up action]
3. [Longer-term action]

Owner for next steps: [name]
Check-in date: [date]

Thank you all for bringing creativity and curiosity today. Design thinking is a practice—the more you use it, the more natural it becomes.

Any final questions?"
```

## Handling Common Facilitation Challenges

### When discussion goes off-track
```
"This is a great discussion, and I want to capture it. [Write on parking lot]
Let's bring our focus back to [current activity]. We can revisit this in [next phase/after workshop]."
```

### When one person dominates
```
"Great input, [name]. Let's hear from some other voices.
[Look at quiet person] What's your perspective on this?"
```

### When energy is low
```
"Let's take a 5-minute energizer break. Stand up, stretch, grab a snack.
When we return, we'll [next activity]."
```

### When there's conflict on direction
```
"I'm hearing two strong perspectives. Let's use dot voting to gather input from everyone.
Write your preference on a sticky note and place it on the spectrum."
```

### When people jump to solutions
```
"That's a great solution idea—let me capture it for later. [Write on parking lot]
Right now we're in [current phase]. We'll get to solutions in ideation."
```

### When someone says "we tried that before"
```
"Tell me more about what happened. What did you learn from that attempt?
What's different now that might change the outcome?"
```
